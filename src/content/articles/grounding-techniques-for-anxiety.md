Anxiety can make the world feel like it's moving too fast to keep up with. Grounding techniques work by redirecting attention to the present moment through the senses — an anchor when thoughts are racing ahead to "what if."

## 1. The 5-4-3-2-1 Technique

Name five things you can see, four you can touch, three you can hear, two you can smell, and one you can taste. This slows the nervous system down by giving it something concrete to do.

## 2. Box Breathing

Inhale for four counts, hold for four, exhale for four, hold for four. Repeat for a few cycles.

## 3. Cold Water

Splashing cold water on the face or holding an ice cube activates the body's dive reflex, which can lower heart rate quickly.

## 4. Naming the Room

Describe your physical surroundings out loud or in your head — colors, shapes, textures. This pulls attention out of the anxious thought loop and into the present.

## 5. Feet on the Floor

Press both feet firmly into the ground and notice the sensation. This is a fast, discreet option that works anywhere, including in a meeting or classroom.

These techniques don't resolve the underlying source of anxiety, but they can help create enough space to think clearly and decide on a next step — including reaching out for support if anxiety is a regular part of your life.
